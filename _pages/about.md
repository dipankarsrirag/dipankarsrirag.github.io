I am a <b>Researcher</b> at <a href="https://www.unsw.edu.au/"><b>UNSW Sydney</b></a>. My research interests broadly lie in <b>socio-culturally inclusive language technologies</b>.

My journey into research began with my master’s thesis, advised by <a href="https://www.unsw.edu.au/staff/aditya-joshi"><b>Dr. Aditya Joshi</b></a>, where I worked on improving dialect robustness in decoder models. During that time, I was fortunate to collaborate with researchers from IIT-Bombay and Google DeepMind.

My current role as a researcher is funded by a <a href="https://research.google/programs-and-events/research-scholar-program/"><b>Google Research Scholar grant</b></a>. Lately, I’ve been building a <b>benchmark for sentiment and sarcasm classification for varieties of English</b>. I also tutor sometimes (:teacher:), which keeps things fun.

Outside work, I’ve been making a conscious effort to touch grass occasionally. I play a lot of FIFA (or is it EA FC now??), watch a lot of anime, and read a lot of manga.

If you want to chat about my work, your work, or why starting One Piece might be the worst decision of your life, feel free to reach out to me on <a href="https://twitter.com/dipankarsrirag" title="X"><i class="fa-brands fa-x-twitter"></i></a>.
