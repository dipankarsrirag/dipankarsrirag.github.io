I am a Ph.D. candidate at the [UNSW-NLP group](https://unswnlp.github.io/) at [University of New South Wales](https://www.unsw.edu.au/), advised by [Dr. Aditya Joshi](https://www.unsw.edu.au/staff/aditya-joshi/), [Dr. Padmanesan Narasimhan](https://www.unsw.edu.au/staff/padmanesan-narasimhan), and [Dr. Salil Kanhere](https://www.unsw.edu.au/staff/salil-kanhere), and funded by the [NHMRC Ideas grant](https://www.nhmrc.gov.au/funding/find-funding/ideas-grants).

My Ph.D. project develops multilingual conversational agents for emergency triage, grounded in real electronic health records. I am currently exploring [simultaneous machine translation](https://aclanthology.org/P19-1289) for [dialects of Arabic](https://glottolog.org/resource/languoid/id/arab1395) as a core component of this work.

My broader research background is in dialect robustness: I have built benchmarks and parameter-efficient adaptation methods that evaluate and close the performance gap NLP models exhibit on non-standard English varieties, focusing on sentiment, sarcasm, slang, and conversation understanding across Australian, Indian, and British English.
